Weight Gain

Many parents fear that too much weight gain during pregnancy is a bad thing. Some feel it is weight they will have to try and lose after the pregnancy, others feel it will lead to a heavier baby and a challenging delivery. With an analysis of the CDC data, we can look to see if there is any coorrelation between the weight gain during pregnancy and larger babies.

BMI

There is a perception that the size of the mother translates to the size of her newborn. The CDC data includes BMI information for the mother which we can compare to the weight of the newborn. BMI is a good measurement as it takes into consideration both the height and weight of the person. Sadly, the CDC data does not include the length of the newborn so the closest attribute we can compare against is weight of the newborn.
